111 years. Four generations.
One river at a time.

EST. 1915 · SEATTLE, WASHINGTON

The history of Wooldridge Boats and company founder Glen Wooldridge has been chronicled in books, magazines, and films since 1915. Florence Arman's book, "The Rogue, a River to Run," gathers up many of the adventures, along with historical photos and facts — with a foreword by Ted Trueblood, the famed associate editor of Field & Stream, who ran rivers and fished with Wooldridge.

It was 1915 when "Grandpa" Glen Wooldridge (1896–1986) built his first river boat to make the first-ever float down Oregon's Rogue River. That trip was just one of Glen's many first-evers. The adventures ranged from Alaska's Yukon to California's Klamath, through the powerful and intimidating Idaho rivers and waters seldom touched in British Columbia. At age 79, Grandpa Glen was the first to run the fearsome Hells Gate on BC's Fraser River.

Ginger Rogers, Clark Gable, Herbert Hoover, Zane Grey, and countless others — famous and not — chose Glen Wooldridge for their fishing and adventure trips. At the root of it all was a Wooldridge boat.

Four generations later the family still builds every boat the same way — welded aluminum, built to order, built to last — from our factory and showroom in Seattle.

Building boats of the highest caliber in quality, value, and performance — that's been the mission for a century and counting.

  • 1915

    Glen Wooldridge builds his first river boat and makes the first-ever float down Oregon's Rogue River. The company is born.

  • 1920s

    Glen takes a boat through the Coffee Pot in the early 1920s — photographed by Louise Stokes of the Grants Pass Courier.

  • 1940

    Glen develops a method of stacking wood strips over a form, allowing better hull shapes without sacrificing strength. That boat is still on display at Rand Ranger Station.

  • 1947

    Running the Rogue — the first-ever trip up the Rogue River (with a prop, of course; no jets back then).

  • 1948

    Glen and Ruell Hawkins make the first trip up Idaho's Salmon River — "The River of No Return."

  • 1949

    Glen starts a boat tour from the city park to Hell Gate: a 50-mile run in a 26-foot boat at $5.50 a person.

  • 1975

    At age 79, Grandpa Glen is first to run Hells Gate on BC's Fraser River.

  • 1979

    Four generations of boat builders working side by side.

  • 2010

    The Wooldridge family — leading the way in custom aluminum boats.

  • 2026

    The 2026 fleet: 18 production models from a 16' tiller sled to a 26' offshore boat, all still built in Seattle.
